The Big Picture

This is where it started. The seed collection that defined the knowledge base's focus:

The core thesis emerged: Data infrastructure isn't AI-ready. Not because of scale or quality, but because of meaning. Semantic layers, ontologies, and context engineering aren't nice-to-haves—they're the missing infrastructure layer.

Three foundational threads:

  1. The semantic gap — Multiple authors identified the same problem: AI can't "read the room" because our data lacks semantic context.
  2. Jessica Talisman's knowledge engineering curriculum — A comprehensive series on controlled vocabularies, ontologies, and metadata modeling. This became the backbone.
  3. dbt's semantic layer documentation — The practical implementation of semantic infrastructure.

Cross-Cutting Insights

1. The semantic gap is real. Every author in this batch, from different angles, identified the same problem: our data infrastructure lacks semantic context. AI doesn't fail because of data quality—it fails because of missing meaning.

2. Jessica Talisman is the curriculum. Her controlled vocabulary → ontology → metadata modeling progression is the most complete treatment of knowledge engineering fundamentals I've found. Start here.

3. dbt's semantic layer is the implementation. The theory (Talisman, Vashishta) meets practice (dbt). For practitioners, this is where to start.

4. Context engineering is the discipline. The term "context engineering" appears across multiple sources. It's the emerging discipline that bridges data engineering, knowledge engineering, and AI.

5. Library science has answers. The "reference interview" insight is profound: librarians solved the "understand what the user really needs" problem decades ago. We're just now applying it to AI.

The Foundation Thesis

This collection established the thesis that drives the entire knowledge base:

Making data AI-ready isn't about pipelines or storage—it's about encoding meaning. The missing infrastructure layer is semantic: ontologies, controlled vocabularies, and context engineering that make data understandable to reasoning systems.

Everything that follows builds on this foundation.
