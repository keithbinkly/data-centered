The Synthesis

This small batch punches above its weight because it captures a moment of clarity in the semantic discourse.

The debate crystallized:

  • MotherDuck asks: "Do we even need this complexity?"
  • Shopify's success answers: "Yes, if you're building for scale"
  • Vashishta warns: "But most attempts will fail anyway"
  • Bergevin explains: "Because the tooling sucks"

The metric tree vs map question is a microcosm of the whole debate:

  • Trees are simpler, hierarchical, easier to understand
  • Maps are richer, handle cross-cutting concerns, but harder to navigate
  • The answer: trees for drill-down, maps for discovery

Context graphs emerged as the bridge concept—neither pure knowledge graph (too academic) nor pure semantic layer (too metrics-focused). It's "the graph of everything relevant to this decision."

Key Tension

The "Figma moment" thesis is the most actionable insight here. Bergevin argues we're at the "hand-coding HTML" stage of semantic infrastructure:

  • Every semantic model is bespoke
  • No visual tools for non-technical stakeholders
  • No version control patterns for semantic assets
  • No marketplace for reusable components

Whoever builds the "Figma for semantics" unlocks the next wave.
